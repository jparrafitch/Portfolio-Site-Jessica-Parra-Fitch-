My Story

Hi, I'm Jessica Parra-Fitch, a UX designer and researcher based in San Diego. When I design my primary motivation is understanding user needs in order to improve products and services that are highly sought after and valued. Learning to create inclusive, accessible and user-centric human experiences has been a fulfilling process highly influenced by my previous professional lives, all of which required the art of empathetic problem-solving that is essential in UX design.

Before I stepped in the UX design sphere I worked as an Account Executive working to bolster brand awareness and sales while strengthening business partnerships through education and inclusion. In my design work I have leveraged this background in sales strategy, product development, and marketing.

An essential skill in human-centered UX design is empathetic problem-solving. I am grateful I had the opportunity to develop these skills as a former massage therapist and community organizer/policy advocate at various environmental justice organizations. I like to contribute to projects, organizations and companies that aim to put out products and services that are socially and environmentally conscious.

In my spare time I like to immerse myself in beautifully engaging experiences, be they in nature, a musical performance, a community gathering or an art show. I try to channel the inspiration I gain from them into my own work with the hope of creating user experiences that can help or inspire others the way they help and inspire me.
